§ 01 — Overview

Introduction

Business, while being a straightforward source of income, is among the most intricate ventures to initiate. The global pandemic compelled people to adapt by delving into buying and selling, exchanging goods, and establishing small businesses. Although some pandemic-launched businesses flourished with soaring sales, many succumbed due to a lack of business acumen.

To thrive in the business realm, critical considerations include the line of business, capital/budget, workforce/human resources, business permits, target market, and location.

core concepts
§ 02 — Core Framework

Business Fundamentals

1

Line of Business (LOB)

Determine the specific type of business you intend to establish — Food & Beverage, Law Firm, Retail, Real Estate, Car Rentals, and so on.

Keep it simple. Align your LOB with your interests — engaging in something you are highly interested in makes the task much easier.
2

Budget

  • Capital — Currency earmarked for tools, equipment, interior design, raw materials, and goods. Restaurants, for example, need funds for tables, chairs, appliances, and raw food.
  • Labor / Human Resources — It is nearly impossible to operate a business single-handedly. Budget for compensation unless embarking on a very small solo venture like food stalls or freelancing.
  • Business Permits — Various licenses such as Mayor's Permit, BIR, DTI, etc. These will be covered further in this lesson.
3

Target Market

Always recognize and understand your market. Ensure you are aware of their potential interests, their willingness to pay for your service or product, and consider factors such as their culture and religion.

4

Location

The location of your establishment is as important as your budget. Key considerations:

  • Accessibility — How easy is it for your target audience to access your store?
  • Distance — Position your business in close proximity to your target market for maximum convenience.
  • Safe Zone — Always prioritize the safety of your customers. Ensure the area is secure for leisure.
business types
§ 03 — Industry Overview

Lines of Business

While there are various types of businesses, here are some commonly owned enterprises:

🍽️ F&B

Food & Beverage

Involves the preparation and serving of food and drinks, from casual eateries and fast-food joints to fine dining restaurants.

🏪 RETAIL

Retail

Sale of goods or services directly to the end consumer. The final stage in the distribution chain — brick-and-mortar or online.

👗 SPECIALTY

Specialty Retail

A niche segment focused on a specific and distinct range of products — boutiques, artisanal shops, gourmet stores, and niche bookshops.

🛒 E-COM

E-Commerce

Buying and selling goods and services over the internet. Covers B2C and B2B transactions through digital platforms and websites.

🏠 PROPERTY

Real Estate Rentals

Leasing of residential and commercial spaces to tenants in exchange for periodic payments. Covers houses, offices, retail, and industrial facilities.

§ 05 — Legal Requirements

Business Permits & Licenses

Starting a business involves various permits and licenses to ensure compliance with health, safety, and operational regulations. The specific requirements vary by location — always check with local authorities. Below is a general list for reference (illustrated using a restaurant example):

PERMIT 01

Business License

General requirement for operating any business; obtained from the local city or county government.

PERMIT 02

Food Service Establishment Permit

Issued by the health department to ensure compliance with food safety regulations.

PERMIT 03

Alcohol License

Required if you plan to serve alcoholic beverages. Type varies (beer & wine, liquor, etc.).

PERMIT 04

Food Handler's Permit

Required for employees who handle or prepare food, demonstrating knowledge of safe food handling.

PERMIT 05

Occupancy Permit

Ensures the restaurant's occupancy capacity complies with local building and safety codes.

PERMIT 06

Sign Permit

Required if you plan to install signage, to ensure compliance with local regulations.

PERMIT 07

Grease Trap Permit

Required if your business disposes of grease waste to prevent environmental issues.

PERMIT 08

Health & Safety Inspections

Regular inspections by the health department to ensure compliance with food safety standards.

PERMIT 09

Music License

If you play music in your establishment, a license may be needed to comply with copyright laws.

PERMIT 10

Employee Identification Number (EIN)

Required for tax purposes, especially if you have employees.

PERMIT 11

Building Permit

Needed for any construction or renovation work on the business premises.

PERMIT 12

Environmental Permits

May be required depending on location and size, related to waste disposal and environmental impact.

PERMIT 13

Fire Department Permits

Ensures compliance with fire safety regulations, including fire exits and suppression systems.

PERMIT 14

Waste Disposal Permits

Required for proper disposal of food waste and other materials generated by the business.

PERMIT 15

Outdoor Seating Permit

Required if you plan to offer outdoor seating, obtained from local authorities.

PERMIT 16

Special Events Permit

May be required if you plan to host special events or promotions at your establishment.

It is crucial to research the specific requirements in your local area and consult with relevant government agencies to ensure you have all necessary permits and licenses to operate legally.
