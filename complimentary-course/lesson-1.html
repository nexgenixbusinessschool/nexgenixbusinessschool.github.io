§ 01 — Overview

Introduction

Business, while being a straightforward source of income, is among the most intricate ventures to initiate. The global pandemic — especially impacting the Philippines — compelled people to adapt by delving into buying and selling, exchanging goods, and establishing small businesses.

With restrictions on outdoor activities, Filipinos sought alternative ways to generate income while avoiding COVID-19 exposure. Although some pandemic-launched businesses flourished with soaring sales, many succumbed due to a lack of business acumen.

To thrive in the business realm, critical considerations include the line of business, capital/budget, workforce/human resources, business permits, target market, and location.
core framework
§ 02 — Core Framework

Business Fundamentals

1

Line of Business (LOB)

Determine the specific type of business you intend to establish — Food & Beverage, Law Firm, Retail, Real Estate, Car Rentals, etc.

Keep it simple and align your LOB with your interests. Passion makes execution far easier.
2

Budget

  • ACapital — Money earmarked for tools, equipment, interior design, raw materials, and goods.
  • BLabor / Human Resources — Budget for compensation. It is nearly impossible to operate a business single-handedly.
  • CBusiness Permits — Various licenses: Mayor's Permit, BIR, DTI, etc.
3

Target Market

Always recognize and understand your market. Be aware of their interests, their willingness to pay, and consider cultural and religious factors.

4

Location

  • AAccessibility — How easy is it for your audience to reach your store?
  • BDistance — Position near your target market for maximum convenience.
  • CSafe Zone — Always prioritize customer safety at your location.

§ 03 — Industry Overview

Lines of Business

While there are various types of businesses, here are some commonly owned enterprises in the Philippines:

🍽️

Food & Beverage

Preparation and serving of food and drinks — from casual eateries to fine dining restaurants.

🏪

Retail

Sale of goods directly to end consumers. The final stage in the distribution chain — brick-and-mortar or online.

👗

Specialty Retail

A niche segment offering a specific range of products — boutiques, artisanal shops, gourmet stores, niche bookshops.

🛒

E-Commerce

Buying and selling goods over the internet. Covers B2C and B2B transactions through digital platforms.

🏠

Real Estate Rentals

Leasing of residential and commercial properties — houses, offices, retail spaces, and industrial facilities.

§ 04 — Legal Requirements

Business Permits & Licenses

Starting a business involves various permits and licenses. Specific requirements vary by location — always consult local authorities. Below is a general list illustrated using a restaurant as an example:

PERMIT 01

Business License

General requirement from the local city or county government.

PERMIT 02

Food Service Establishment Permit

Issued by the health department for food safety compliance.

PERMIT 03

Alcohol License

Required if serving alcoholic beverages.

PERMIT 04

Food Handler's Permit

Required for employees who handle or prepare food.

PERMIT 05

Occupancy Permit

Ensures occupancy capacity complies with building codes.

PERMIT 06

Sign Permit

Required for installing signage at your establishment.

PERMIT 07

Grease Trap Permit

Required for grease waste disposal to prevent environmental issues.

PERMIT 08

Health & Safety Inspections

Regular health department inspections for food safety.

PERMIT 09

Music License

Required if you play music, for copyright compliance.

PERMIT 10

Employee Identification Number (EIN)

Required for tax purposes when you have employees.

PERMIT 11

Building Permit

Needed for construction or renovation work on premises.

PERMIT 12

Environmental Permits

Related to waste disposal and environmental impact.

PERMIT 13

Fire Department Permits

Ensures compliance with fire safety: exits and suppression systems.

PERMIT 14

Waste Disposal Permits

For proper disposal of food waste and other materials.

PERMIT 15

Outdoor Seating Permit

Required if you plan to offer outdoor seating areas.

PERMIT 16

Special Events Permit

May be required for hosting special events or promotions.
